What We Do

Funding

ISA funds any international-oriented event held on campus. Kindly read the funding requirements below before applying for funding.

Mentorship

MINT pairs incoming international students and underclassmen with student mentors. The program seeks to connect students with the broader international community.

Advocacy

ISA advocates for the needs of international students and for global causes such as the Foreign Student Employment Program and Letters to Home.

Planning

ISA organizes and host our own events aimed towards creating a comfortable atmosphere for people to learn about other cultures and have conversations about current global affairs.

Working with Student Assembly

ISA works closely with Cornell's Student Assembly to allocate funding across international clubs and promote the well-being of international students on campus.

History of ISA

1981

The International Students Association (ISA) was established as the International Students Programming Board (ISPB), later renamed the International Students Board (ISB), an umbrella student organization promoting cross-cultural understanding and international life by programming, sponsoring, and co-sponsoring international activities. The organization originally functioned as a funding board, with an aim to expand to wider-reaching functions.

2014

11.4% of Cornell's undergraduate student population was composed of international students; however, there was no established student organization to represent the general international population. As it became evident that there was a rising need for international students to have the resources to form a stronger on-campus community, this, as well as the rate of success and interest in ISB’s activities, prompted ISB to pass a resolution in the Student Assembly and expand into the International Students Association (ISA).

2015 - 2025

ISB had officially changed its name to ISU, and opened its General Body division to the entire Cornell community. ISU has not only continued its role as a funding organization, but has also been actively addressing the needs and concerns of international students, particularly by promoting internationalism and advocacy on campus.

2025 - Today

ISU has officially changed its name to ISA. More information on this will follow soon.
